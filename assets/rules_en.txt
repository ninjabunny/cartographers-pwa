head|1|Overview
In Cartographers, you earn reputation stars over four seasons exploring the landscapes of Nalos. You will add various terrain in different shapes based on revealed card. Chart the land wisely and fulfill Queen Gimnax's Edicts well to gain the highest reputation.

subhead|2|Seasons
<img src="images/rules/jahresz.png" style="width: 40%; float: left;"/>The game is played over four seasons. Each season consists of several rounds (about 4-8). Explore cards are revealed, which show which areas you can explore by marking the spaces in your map to earn reputation.

subhead|2|Explore cards
<img src="images/rules/form.png" style="width: 20%; float: left;"/><img src="images/rules/type.png" style="width: 20%; float: left;"/>Each revealed explore card tells you what to draw on your map. It will show a shape and the choice of two terrain types, or a terrain type and the choice of two shapes.

After you have made your selection, you can place the shape on your map by tapping on the desired locations.
<img src="images/rules/ruleok.png" style="width: 8%; float: left;"/>If you are satisfied with the placement, confirm it by pressing the "OK" button.

subhead|3|Special Explore Cards
Among the explore cards, you will also find:
<ul>
<li><img src="images/rules/ruine2.png" style="width: 15%; float: right;"/>Ruins: If a ruin card is revealed, another card is immediately revealed. The shape from this card are must be placed so that it covers one of the ruin spaces on the map.</li>
<li><img src="images/rules/monster.png" style="width: 15%; float: right;"/>Ambush: If an ambush card is revealed, the shape of monster spaces on the card are automatically placed and will add negative reputation to your score for each open, adjacent space around them during scoring. (see Scoring)</li>
</ul>

subhead|2|Game Phases
<img src="images/rules/time.png" style="width: 8%; float: right;"/>As each explore card is revealed, shapes are added to the player's map until the season is complete, and scoring occurs. The duration of the season is shown in the upper left corner of the season card (8/8/7/6). When the sum of the time values on the revealed explore cards reaches or exceeds the season duration, the season is complete. Two scoring cards are evaluated at the end of each season, and each is evaluated twice over the course of the game. <br/><img src="images/rules/dekret.png" style="width: 27%; float: left;"/>At the end of the season, the scoring of the Queen's edicts (scoring cards) for the corresponding season follows.<ul><li>Spring - Edict A and B</li><li>Summer - Edict B and C</li><li>Autumn - Edict C and D</li><li>Winter - Edict D and A</li></ul>

head|1|Scoring
<img src="images/rules/gruenfl_en.png" style="width: 27%; float: left;"/>Each scoring card has a unique scoring condition, which is described in detail below. For example, for the "Greenbough" card, you gain one reputation for row or column that has a forest space. (The card example shows gaining 5 reputation)
You can find the full description for each scoring card during gameplay by tapping the desired scoring card.

subhead|2|Monsters and Gold
<img src="images/rules/monsi.png" style="width: 20%; float: left;"/>In addition, at the end of each season, reputation is deducted for each empty space adjacent to a monster space. Surround the monsters with filled spaces to reduce the deductions at the end of each season.
<img src="images/rules/abzug.png" style="width: 27%; float: right;"/>The total number of negative reputation due to monsters is always shown above the map.

<img src="images/rules/muenze.png" style="width: 10%; float: left;"/> As a bonus, you will also receive reputation stars at the end of the season for all of your gold. This value is always shown above the map.
<img src="images/rules/berg.png" style="width: 20%; float: left;"/>You gain gold by either surrounding a mountain space on your map on all four sides with filled spaces, or by selecting and placing the smaller, coin-marked shapes on some explore cards.
Gold awards points at the end of each season for the total amount collected. In this way, a gold collected in the first season will award points in all four seasons.

head|1|Game End
The game ends at the completion of the winter season. Depending on what scoring cards are in play, an amount of reputation is deduced from your final score.
<img src="images/rules/basis.png" style="width: 10%; float:left;"/>Find this deduction on each scoring card in the bottom right corner of the card. These are also listed on the final overview screen during scoring.
<img src="images/rules/wertung.png" style="width: 60%;display:block;margin:auto;"/>
The resulting final score indicates how satisfied Queen Gimnax is with your exploration.

head|1|Heroes
When a hero card is revealed, draw a 1x1 square anywhere on the map, then it will be filled with the hero terrain type.
<img src="images/rules/attack.png" style="height: 2em; float: left;"/><p>The spaces in the depicted attack pattern will be marked, relative to the hero space. Attack patterns can overlap filled spaces and the edge of the map.</p>
Heroes destroy monsters in two ways: When a hero is drawn, all monster spaces within the hero’s attack pattern <img src="images/rules/attack.png" style="height: 1em; margin: 0px;"/> are immediately destroyed.
Then, for the rest of the game, all monster spaces drawn within any hero’s attack pattern are destroyed.

head|1|Destroying Spaces
Some spaces on your map might get destroyed. When a space is destroyed, a large <img src="images/rules/zerstoert.png" style="height: 1em; margin: 0px;"/> is drawn over the space.
Destroyed spaces count as filled spaces, but have no terrain type.
If a hero space is destroyed, that hero’s attack pattern remains active.

head|1|Undercity - ABOVE AND BELOW
<img src="images/rules/undercity.png" style="width: 20%; float: left;"/>The Undercity map sheets have two sections: above ground and below ground, which are separated horizontally by the ground line. These sections are connected by the gate, which is considered to be a filled space.
You may only draw shapes so that they are entirely above ground or below ground. When drawing a shape, you must place it so that a continuous path of filled spaces can be traced from the new shape back to the gate.
If you cannot legally draw any of the available shapes, you instead get a 1x1 square filled with any terrain type (excluding mountains), but they must still be able to trace a continuous path back to the gate.
